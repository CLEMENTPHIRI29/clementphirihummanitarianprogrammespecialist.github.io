Professional Profile

About Clement

Clement Phiri is a humanitarian programme professional with 7 years of direct ECHO/WFP programme management experience in Malawi, complemented by three years of applied water and energy engineering at Davis and Shirtliff. His career is characterised by measurable impact at scale: a USD 10.3 million portfolio, 13,721 direct beneficiaries reached, and a 100% Post-Distribution Monitoring satisfaction score across six districts.

As Programmes Manager at the Foundation for Irrigation and Sustainable Development (FISD), Clement led the full humanitarian programme cycle from needs assessment and beneficiary targeting through to ECHO-compliant donor reporting and risk management. His integrated livelihoods model at Domwe and Mposa in Blantyre District broke the cycle of recurring hunger within two years and was formally adopted by WFP Malawi as a national replication reference.

Since August 2022, Clement has served as Malawi Representative Technical Engineer at Davis and Shirtliff, deepening his expertise in solar irrigation systems, borehole development, water treatment, and renewable energy. This applied engineering experience enables him to independently conduct technical assessments of livelihoods infrastructure assets, reducing programme costs and accelerating implementation timelines.

"The Domwe-Mposa model I developed to break the cycle of recurring hunger was adopted by WFP Malawi as a national model for replication — a result built on addressing root causes, not just symptoms."
